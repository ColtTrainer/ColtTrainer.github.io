Final Boss Fights

What makes them good?

Final boss fights in video games are the the culmination of the entire game, and the player needs to feel that they have reached the end of the game, and are expecting to go out with a bang. The boss needs to test everything they have learned while having a powerful atmosphere while wrapping up the story. When it comes to the fight itself, mechanically it should be one of the more difficult fights in the game to not underwhelm the player experience. Another thing to keep in mind is that it can be easy to make an overwhelming fight just as it is to make and underwhelming one, so the abilities of the boss need to stay reasonably limited.

How to compare them

Since games have very different mechanics and styles, narrowing down the best boss fights is down to each person's opinions. To compare bosses, it's combining how well they execute things such as mechanics, atmosphere, narrative, etc. in their own genre. For example, a fast paced shooter game would typically have a fast paced final boss that needs to be shot at and dodged for some time, and epic music that fits the tone of the game is used to hype up the player experience. On the other hand, an adventure game that has a final challenge or boss would have to focus more on the atmosphere, because that is the central part of the game and the player experience, so to judge a good final boss requires to look at the context of the game.

Criteria

These are some criteria I use for judging fights

  • Must be mechanically difficult, fair, and fun to experience
  • Must have a unique design of the boss and the area
  • It makes sense or is satisfying to the story that this is the final boss
  • Music should be good and fitting of the type of fight and story
